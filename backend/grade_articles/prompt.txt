Base your grade **solely** on the information **explicitly stated or clearly implied** within the abstract text provided below. **HAS / Anaes Grading Criteria (Based on Abstract Content):**

*   **Grade A:**
    *   **Basis:** Abstract describes "Preuve scientifique établie" (Established scientific proof).
    *   **Source Evidence (Niveau 1):** The abstract explicitly mentions or clearly describes results derived from:
        *   High-power randomized controlled trials (RCTs) without major bias (« essais comparatifs randomisés de forte puissance et sans biais majeur »).
        *   Meta-analyses of randomized controlled trials (« méta-analyse d’essais contrôlés randomisés »).

    *   *Note:* If the abstract provides evidence corresponding to Niveau 1, assign Grade A, even if expert opinion is also mentioned.

*   **Grade B:**
    *   **Basis:** Abstract describes "Présomption scientifique" (Scientific presumption).
    *   **Source Evidence (Niveau 2):** The abstract explicitly mentions or clearly describes results derived from:
        *   Low-power RCTs (« essais comparatifs randomisés de faible puissance »).
        *   Well-conducted non-randomized comparative studies (« études comparatives non randomisées bien menées »).
        *   Cohort studies (« études de cohortes »).
    *   **Source Evidence (Niveau 3):** The abstract explicitly mentions or clearly describes results derived from:
        *   Case-control studies (« études cas-témoins »).
    *   *Note:* An abstract describing any study type from Niveau 2 OR Niveau 3 falls under Grade B, provided it doesn't meet Grade A criteria.

*   **Grade C:**
    *   **Basis:** Abstract describes "Faible niveau de preuve scientifique" (Low level of scientific proof).
    *   **Source Evidence (Niveau 4):** The abstract explicitly mentions or clearly describes results derived from:
        *   Comparative studies with major biases explicitly mentioned or strongly implied (« études comparatives comportant des biais importants »).
        *   Retrospective studies (« études rétrospectives »).
        *   Case series (« séries de cas »).
        *   Descriptive epidemiological studies (cross-sectional, longitudinal) (« études épidémiologiques descriptives (transversale, longitudinale) »).
    *   *Note:* Assign Grade C if the evidence described fits Niveau 4 and doesn't meet Grade A or B criteria.

*   **Accord d'experts (Expert Agreement):**
    *   **Basis:** Assigned **only** when the abstract **lacks sufficient information** to determine the study type or level of evidence required for Grades A, B, or C according to the definitions above. This grade signifies the absence of reported scientific study data (RCT, cohort, case-control, meta-analysis, etc.) within the abstract to support a specific grade (A, B, or C). It is the default if the abstract primarily presents opinions, non-systematic reviews without clear methodology, or findings without detailing the underlying study design that generated them.
